## Research
My research integrates evolutionary genomics, landscape genetics, and community ecology to understand how wildlife respond to global change and to assist conservation practitioners in navigating threats to species persistence. Working primarily with wide-ranging carnivores, I investigate mechanisms through which genetic diversity is created, maintained, lost, and restored. My goal is to identify populations at risk, predict adaptive potential, and inform conservation strategies that maintain biodiversity while mitigating human–wildlife conflict.
